Experiment 2 — SQNR vs Crest Factor

Single-spike injection model · [64×64] Gaussian weight matrices
20 seeds averaged · κ_nat ≈ 4.08 for N=4096

Crest Factor Definition: For X ∈ ℝk with i.i.d. Xi ~ N(0, σ²), block RMS = σ, and κ := max(|X|) / σ. The natural κ of a pure Gaussian block with N=4096 elements is κ_nat ≈ √(2 ln N) ≈ 4.08. This experiment injects a single outlier of magnitude κ·σ into an otherwise N(0,1) background and sweeps κ from 2.0 to 100.0. For κ ≫ κ_nat the injected spike dominates; for κ ≲ κ_nat the background peak sets the effective crest factor.

Theoretical predictions:
  • INT(T): scale ∝ κ → step size ∝ κ → SQNR ∝ −20 log₁₀(κ) (steepest drop)
  • INT(C): only the outlier's row is affected; 63/64 rows remain at full quality
  • MXINT/MXFP: only the outlier's block (32 elements) is coarsened; rest at full quality
  • HAD+INT(C): per-row HAD spreads the spike uniformly; effective κ_had ≈ √(κ²+N)/max_had → slowly growing
  • SQ-Format: outlier element wins a high-precision slot; SQNR nearly flat
